Blogs

Startup Playbook

This is meant for people new to the world of startups. Most of this will not be new to people who have read a lot of what YC partners have written—the goal is to get it into one place.

Paul Graham

Contains essays from Paul Graham about many topics from various fields from tech to startups to some abstract ideas.

Y Combinator

  • Y Combinator Startup Library.
  • Y Combinator’s Essential Startup Advice.

Dan Zarrella's Blog

HubSpot’s “social media scientist” has one of the most widely respected personal marketing blogs. He doesn’t publish new content that frequently, but when he does it’s indispensable. Example: “Tweets Between 100 and 115 Characters are More Likely to be ReTweeted.”

Technology & Marketing Law Blog

An award-winning blog on internet law, intellectual property, and advertising law by law professor Eric Goldman.

Farnam Street

Farnam Street (FS) is devoted to helping you develop an understanding of how the world really works, make better decisions, and live a better life. They address such topics as mental models, decision making, learning, reading, and the art of living.

Likelihood of Confusion

Likelihood of Confusion on internet trademark and copyright infringement by lawyer Ron Coleman.

Mashtag Blawg

Mashtag Blawg by Bottom Line Law Group, a firm specializing in lean startups and business growth.

Walker Corporate Law

Walker Corporate Law Blog by a boutique firm that specializes in representing entrepreneurs.

Heidi Cohen's Blog

Heidi Cohen’s “actionable marketing guide” offers useful and instructive columns on social media marketing, content marketing, mobile marketing, and branding.

Pmarchive

An archive of the best articles from Marc Andreessen's original blog.pmarca.com.

The Ken

In depth articles on startup, tech, healthcare etc. A must for every startup enthusiast.

AVC

Is a blog by Fred Wilson is an American businessman, venture capitalist and blogger.

Podcasts

The Joe Rogan Experience

The Joe Rogan Experience is a free audio and video podcast hosted by American comedian, actor, sports commentator, and television host Joe Rogan.

The Tim Ferriss Show

Tim Ferriss is a self-experimenter and bestselling author, best know for The 4 - Hour Workweek. In this show, he deconstructs world-class performers from eclectic areas(investing, chess, pro sports, ect.), digging deep to find the tools, tactics and tricks that listeners can use.

Masters of scale

Masters of Scale is an original podcast hosted by Reid Hoffman, Co-founder of LinkedIn and Investor at Greylock. In each episode, Reid shows how companies grow from zero to a gazillion, testing his theories with legendary leaders.

DRT.fm - Dorm Room tycoon

Dorm Room Tycoon is a podcast show for designers, developers, growth hackers and founders. Each interview cuts to the chase, uncovering unique and practical insights. The show doesn’t celebrate the guests’ accomplishments or take a trip down memory lane; rather the show is concerned about startup lessons and first principles.

How to start a startup - YC/Stanford

Sam Altman and the folks from Y Combinator offer up an amazing course in "How To Start A Startup" at Stanford. Course includes lectures from: Sam Altman, Dustin Moskovitz, Paul Graham, Adora Cheung, Peter Thiel, Alex Schultz, Kevin Hale and many more.

The knowledge project with Shane Parrish

The Knowledge Project Podcast explores the ideas, methods, and mental models, that help expand your mind, live deliberately, and master the best of what other people have already figured out.

How I Built This with Guy Raz

Guy Raz dives into the stories behind some of the world's best known companies. How I Built This weaves a narrative journey about innovators, entrepreneurs and idealists—and the movements they built.

Product Hunt Radio

A weekly podcast with the people creating and exploring the future. Tune in every week with Ryan Hoover as he's joined by founders, investors, journalists, and makers to discuss the latest in tech.

Gimlet - StartUp

StartUp is an American podcast from Gimlet Media hosted by Alex Blumberg and Lisa Chow. They cover variety of topics from stories of starting businesses to making a TV show and business in general.

a16z Podcast

The a16z Podcast discusses tech and culture trends, news, and the future -- especially as ‘software eats the world’. It features industry experts, business leaders, and other interesting thinkers and voices from around the world.

SaaStr Podcasts

The Official Saastr Podcast is the latest and greatest from the world of Saastr, interviewing the most prominent operators and investors to discover their tips, tactics and strategies to attain success in the fiercely competitive world of SaaS.

Books

Outliers, the story of success

When a journalist looks for facts and connections between people who are a huge success, the outcome is always interesting. Malcolm Gladwell wrote his third book ‘Outliers: The story of success’ after extensive research and many interviews.

Rich Dad Poor Dad

Robert Kiyosaki’s book breaks down everything you need to know about financial education without giving you a headache.The book will shine a light on the 9-5 job and the rat race that keeps you in that job you hate in order to pay the debts.

The Lean Startup

How Today's Entrepreneurs Use Continuous Innovation to Create Radically Successful Businesses by Eric Ries.

The Entrepreneur Mind

100 Essential Beliefs, Characteristics, and Habits of Elite Entrepreneurs by Kevin Johnson.

The 4-Hour Workweek

Escape 9-5, Live Anywhere, and Join the New Rich by Thomas Ferriss.

Founders at Work

Stories of Startups' Early Days by Jessica Livingston.

Funding

ValueWalk

Started in 2010, ValueWalk.com offers breaking financial industry news with a focus on hedge funds, large asset managers, and value investing. The site provides quality content that is important to value investors (most of which is free).

SumZero

This site describes itself as “the world’s largest community exclusively for professional investors, providing quality, peer-reviewed investment research from top analysts and rising stars in the fund industry.”

Y Combinator Financing Documents

This contains sample forms for raising equity rounds with angel investors.

Zero Hedge

Zero Hedge is written by multiple authors under pseudonyms. This highly influential blog includes both aggregated and original financial news and opinion. Zero Hedge’s mission is to increase the public’s awareness of financial, economic, and political issues, while also educating readers to make informed decisions.

Moneyness

Moneyness is an economics blog by JP Koning, which covers economics, monetary policy, central banking, and finance. He adds a unique perspective to money-related issues and explains complex ideas with clarity and ease. This is a great blog for anyone interested in finance and monetary policy.

Marginal Revolution

Founded by Professors Tyler Cowen and Alex Tabarrok of George Mason University, Marginal Revolution is one of the most popular economics blogs on the web. Content includes articles, links, commentaries, and fascinating debates on the real world implications of economics issues.

Courses / MOOCs

New Venture Finance: Startup Funding For Entrepreneurs (Coursera)

Professor Michael R. Pratt from University of Maryland has got you covered right from prepping for funding, to whom to approach, how to pitch and how to close the funding process. This course will be an eye-opener for all entrepreneurs who think that the funding process starts and ends with a good pitch presentation and a stunning business plan.

Successful Negotiation: Essential Strategies and Skills (Coursera)

George Siedel, in collaboration with the University of Michigan, has put together more than eight hours of practical, usable information that breaks down the process of negotiation for you.
